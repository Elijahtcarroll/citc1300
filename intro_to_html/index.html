Galaxies

Galaxies are spread all throughout outer space, each containing billions of stars. Our galaxy, The Milky Way, is one of numerous in the universe. Galaxies vary depending on size and shape, some form to look like spirals while others take on more of an oval shape. Studying galaxies helps astronomers understand the structure and evolution of the universe. Studies have shown that the Andromeda Galaxy, one of our closest neighboring galaxy, will collide with our galaxy in six billion years.

Exoplanets

Exoplanets are any planets that orbit beyond our solar system. Most exoplanets orbit other stars but some are free-floating exoplanets which are called "Rogue Planets". The first exoplanets were discovered in the 1990s. There are many different types of planets ranging from gas giants bigger than Jupiter to small rocky planets smaller than Earth. Some Exoplanets can be covered in lava while others are completely frozen.

Types of Exoplanets

  • Gas Giants
  • Neptunian planets
  • Super-Earths
  • Terrestrial Planets
